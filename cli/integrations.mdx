xCommand is designed to seamlessly blend into your existing engineering environment. Whether running locally on your machine or deployed on a remote VM, it acts as an agentic layer that leverages the tools you already use every day.

## Human-Like Problem Solving

xCommand doesn't just run commands; it understands your intent. With appropriate permissions, it can act like a human expert—investigating logs, debugging infrastructure, and performing system operations using the native tools already installed on your machine or VM.

## Phased Beta Approach

We are currently in the **beta phase**, taking a deliberate and phased approach to building out native integrations. Our goal is to ensure each connection is robust, secure, and truly enhances your engineering workflow.

### Current Support

Currently, we support core lifecycle and communication integrations:

- [**Slack**](/cli/integrations/slack): Connect your xCommand agents to Slack to receive notifications and interact via slash commands.
- [**GitHub**](/cli/integrations/github): Manage repositories, PRs, issues, and workflows directly from the CLI.
- **Environment Native**: xCommand integrates directly with your local shell, environment variables, and pre-installed CLI tools (like `gcloud`, `aws-cli`, `kubectl`) to manage your platform as you would.

### Roadmap

In future phases, we will be expanding native support for:

- **Incident Management**: PagerDuty, Incident.io
- **Project Tracking**: Linear, Jira
- **Documentation**: Notion, Confluence
- **Cloud Monitoring**: Datadog, New Relic

We are building the future of autonomous cloud management one integration at a time. If there is a specific tool you'd like to see prioritized, please let us know!
